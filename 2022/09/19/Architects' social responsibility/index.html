Architects' social responsibility

Created2022-09-19|Updated2022-12-16|Reading and writing
|Post View:

Looking back at the achievements of architects in the twentieth century to see the social responsibility of architects, there are three significant directions to discuss: (1) essential spatial aesthetics: the spirit of place and the creation of spatial aesthetics (2) Social architectural field initiatives: solving housing problems and housing policies (3) The preservation of architectural heritage accumulated by history: the rise and fall of capitalist cities The enthusiasm that architects have professionally cultivated has probably been most predominant in the creation of essential spatial aesthetics. Especially in the ‚ÄúAesthetics of Modernity‚Äù developed in the 20th century, architects played a vital role in Cubism, Bauhaus, and Futurism initiatives. Secondly, the social architectural field finally became a living reality. After the two world wars, countries recovered and rebuilt from the ashes of the war. Residential design has emerged as the most critical issue in urban development. It became an essential social conviction for architects to initiate a modern living style that meets social reality, historical tradition, and climatic conditions. Under the dominance of capitalist society and the consumer lifestyle, urban decay and redevelopment have eventually become the most crucial policy slogans! In contrast, architects still need to develop their more important social initiatives in this issue of historical accumulation, especially cultural assets and urban preservation.

It has been 18 years since we entered the 21st century, and many challenges have been encountered by humanity in this century, all of which are global issues, so the social responsibility of architects has changed and become more critical. The ultimate responsibility is the sustainability of the environment and the planet. There are seven themes related to a sustainable environment and human settlements: mobility, resources, participation, community, eco-resorts, regeneration, and digital affirmative action. Televillages) can consider how to practice sustainability issues practically. Accordingly, the social responsibility and the role of architects in the 21st century will be defined. There are five points of explanation: (1) Environment and urbanism in its landscapeArchitecture should be seen as an ecological infrastructure that integrates natural processes within the urban fabric and creates public infrastructure as a combination of diverse ecological and architectural forms. Architects must work with landscape architects to create such environments. (2) Environmental innovation for energy savings and carbon reduction, focusing on green and low-carbon urban design, maintaining the value of public space, and green base plates. (3) Architects should investigate various carbon reduction design possibilities for environmental awareness and building technology innovation, not just for achieving a green label. (4) The relationship between regional environment and rural regenerationGeographical environment is the concept of regional structure centered on settlements, combined with cultural regeneration as a natural combination of tradition and innovation. (5) The design plan (program) for the preservation of the historic environment and architecture should incorporate the principles of preservation of tangible cultural assets of the United Nations Educational, Scientific, and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) for the preservation and maintenance of the historic environment. The social responsibility of architects in the 21st century, a role that encompasses the duality of preservation and innovation, is indeed significant and challenging.
